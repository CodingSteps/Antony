Dinosaurs

What are dinosaurs?

Dinosaurs are huge creatures that existed millions of years ago. They varied in size, shape and colour. Some can even reach the size of tall buildings! Just like modern-day animals, these creatures were also divided into herbivores, carnivores, omnivores and scavengers. Different dinosaurs lived in different continents, even Antarctica!

When did they appear?

They appeared in the Mesozoic Era which began 252 MYA and ended 65 MYA. The Mesozoic Era was divided into three Periods:- Triassic, Jurassic and Cretaceous Period which are further divided into Early, Middle and Late. The below table shows the timeline of the Mesozoic Era.

MESOZOIC ERA
Triassic Jurassic Cretacous
Early Middle Late Early Middle Late Early Late
252 to 247 MYA 247 to 237 MYA 237 to 201 MYA 201 to 174 MYA 174 to 164 MYA 164 to 145 MYA 145 to 100 MYA 100 to 65 MYA

How was the earth like when they existed?

In the Triassic Period, the earth had a supercontinent namd Pangaea. All the continents were connected to each other. But at that time India was not with Asia that time. Also, at that time, there was only one ocean, the Tethys Ocean. Also, Pangaea had no ice caps and the land was hot and dry .Therefore, Pangaea was dominated by large areas of desert. The name comes from two Greek words "pan" and "gaia" which means "All Earth". There were ony brown and green plants and trees that time like Cycad, Gingko leaves, Monkey puzzle and Horsetail.


In the Jurassic Period, Pangaea seperated into 2 smaller supercontinents, Laurasia and Gondwana. Laurasia consists of Eurasia and North America and Gondwana consists of Africa, South America, Australia, Antarctica and even India, which was still not with Asia yet.


In the Cretaceous Period, all the continents finally seperated from each, but they were not in their shapes we see today yet. And still, India was not connected to Asia, but was moving towards it. Also, 3 more oceans appeared - Pacific, Arctic and Atlantic Ocean. Later, after the dinosaurs went extinct, India finally came in contact with Asia and the Tethys Ocean disappeared and the Southern and Indian Ocean appeared as we can see today. This change of landmass happened due to the tectonic plates which always move, therefore, changing the shape of the land. This is when the first flowers appeared like magnolias and passion flowers.


How do we come to know about dinosaurs?

The first dinosaur fossils were recognized in the early 19th century, and in 1841, Sir Richard Owen coined the name 'Dinosaur' which means 'Terrible Lizard'. Since then, dinosaur fossils had been major attractions at museums worldwide and books about them appeared and movies like "Jurassic Park" came. We also know about dinosaurs from fossil evidence.

Sir Richard Owen


Types of Dinosaurs

Dinosaurs are divided into two types based on their hip bones: Saurischians (Lizard-hipped) and Ornithischians (Bird-hipped). Saurischians had a pair of hip bones that pointed forwards or down. Ornithischians had a pair of hip bones pointing back. All theropods are saurischians, but sauropods were saurischians as well. Ornithischians were all herbivores. Everything was normal until 2018 when all the theropods (except for herrerasaurids) were put in a newly-made group called Ornithoscelida along with Ornithischians as they were closely related to Ornithischia. Theropods

Carnivorous Dinosaurs were known as "Theropods" which means "Beast-Footed". Some dinosaurs hunted alone while some hunted in packs. However, not all of them were carnivores. Some were omnivores or herbivores as well. Let's look at some of them here.


Dromaeosaurs

Dromaeosaurs were raptors which were two-legged and carnivorous. They had scales and some even had feathers! They hunted in packs so that they can bring down large herbivores as well. The following dinosaurs here are dromaeosaurs.

Velociraptor

Velociraptor means "Speedy Thief". It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in China, Russia and Mongolia. It was 5-6 feet long and weighed 15 kgs. It had 80 sharp bladed teeth. It had large eyes which gave it excellent vision in the dark. It hunted in packs so it could bring down large herbivores. It ran 37 mph so it can outrun its prey. It also had a sickle-shaped claw on its feet which could be used to stab its prey causing the prey much blood loss which could lead to death. Before stabbing its prey, it jumped at a height of 12 feet off the ground to pounce and attack. There was even a fossil of Velociraptor and Protoceratops fighting in the Gobi Desert. Both of them seemed to have died in a sudden sandstorm, preserving the battle forever.

Velociraptor

Fossil of Velociraptor and Protoceratops fighting



Dromaeosaurus

Dromaeosaurus means "fast running lizard". It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Montana (Western USA) and Alberta (Canada). It was 2 metres long and weighed 15 kgs. It was an active and agile dinosaur. Its tail was stiffenned by a lattice of bony rods and was flexible at the base so it could be moved up and down. The tail is used for balance while running. It had a large head, containing a big brain. It had excellent vision due to its large eyes and also had a strong sense of smell and good hearing making it an efficient hunter. Its had long, razor sharp teeth. Its bite force was more powerful than velociraptor's. It had sickle-shaped claws on each finger which is used to slash its prey. The claws on the foot were used to do some terrible damage to its prey.

Dromaeosaurus

Deinonychus

Deinonychus means "Terrible Claw". It lived 110 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Southern Montana. It was 10 feet long and weighed 80 kgs. It had a large head with powerful jaws and 60 sharp serrated teeth. It also had excellent eyesight. Its tail was stiff which helped it to balance and attack prey. It had three curved claws on each hand which was long and sharp. They were used to climb trees while escaping from predators. It had 4 toes on each foot with claws at the end. The second claw was 13 cm long which was why Deinonychus got its name. The sickle-shaped claw was used to stab its prey, then waited for it to bleed to death.

Deinonychus

Utahraptor

Utahraptor means "Utah's Predator". It lived 126 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period. As the name implies, its fossils were found in Utah(USA). It was 5 feet tall and weighed 300 kgs to 1 ton. It was the largest dromaeosaur that ever lived. It ran fast like other raptors. It could smell prey fronm 1.5 kms away. Its sickle-shaped second claw was 38 cms long. It attacked its prey with these claws while holding on tight with its strong hands.

Utahraptor


Oviraptorosaurs

Oviraptorosaurs are a group of feathered dinosaurs. They were omnivorous. They look like primitive flightless birds. Their feathers provided good insulation for them. The following dinosaurs here are oviraptorosaurs.


Caudipteryx

Caudipteryx means "Tail feather" or "winged tail". It lived 124.6 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in China. It was 1 metre long and weighed 2.3 kgs. It was the smallest oviraptorosaur that ever lived. It was the size of a turkey. It had long legs, short arms and and a short tail. Its short snout had long sharp teeth. It had long quill-like feathers on its arms and tail. The tail feathers were up to 20 cms long and are arranged like a fan. They used these feathers to attract mates. It ate small fish, plants and small animals. They also swallowed stones called gastroliths to digest food easily.

Caudipteryx

Oviraptor

Oviraptor means "Egg Thief". It lived 80 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Mongolia. It measured 5.2 ft long and weighed 36 kgs. It had short arms for grasping. These arms had three fingers wtih claws up to 8 cms long. It had a flexible neck, long tail and a parrot-shaped jaw. It didn't have any teeth but its jaws were powerful and modified for crushing its food, so it ate meat, plants, insects, small reptiles and shellfish. It also had a crest on its snout to attract females. The reason why this dinosaur was named Oviraptor is because one of its fossils showed that it was near a nest with eggs and scientists thought it was stealing eggs, hence the nameas given. However, later studies have shown that the nest belonged to the Oviraptor itself and was actually protecting its eggs.

Oviraptor

Citipati

Citipati means "Funeral Pyre Lord". It lived 75 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Mongolia. It measured 9.5 ft in length and weighed 75 to 83 kgs. Although it had no teeth, it nipped on leaves, twigs and fruits with its sharp edges of its beak and grabbed small animals with its clawed hands.

Citipati

Gigantoraptor

Gigantoraptor means "giant seizer". It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossis were found in Mongolia. It measured 26 to 29 ft in length and weighed 1.4 to 2.7 tons. It was the largest oviraptorosaur that ever lived. It had elongated arms and legs. It had no teeth, but its jaws were strong to bite plants and meat.

Gigantoraptor


Orinithomimids

Orinithomimids are dinosaurs which resemble modern ostriches. They were omnivores and were very fast. Their skulls were small with large eyes on their long and slender necks. They all had toothless beaks.Their arms were long and had powerful claws. Their legs were long and powerful,. Ornithomimids were the fastest of all dinosaurs. Some had feathers as well. The following dinosaurs were orinithomimids.


Orinithomimus

Orinithomimus means "Bird Mimic". It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Alberta (Canada). It measured 12 ft in length and weighed 170 kgs. It ate insects, crustaceans, fruit, leaves, branches, eggs, lizards and small mammals. They had good eyesight so they were nocturnal as well. It could outrun predators by reaching a speed of more than 35 to 40 mph.

Orinithomimus

Struthiomimus

Struthiomimus means "Ostrich Mimic".It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Alberta (Canada), Coahuila (Mexico) and Saskatchewan (Canada). It measured 19 ft long and weighed 420 kgs. Its legs were long, powerful and well-suited for running like an ostrich. It could run 31 to 50 mph and outrun its predators.

Struthiomimus

Gallimimus

Gallimimus means "Chicken Mimic" or "Rooster Mimic" It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Mongolia. It measured 20 ft in length and weighed 400 to 500 kgs. It was the largest orinithomimid that ever lived. It had a long neck, round head and a toothless beak. It had a long tail which gave it extra balance and helped it to turn while running away. It ran at a speed of 43 mph. It ate insects, lizards, eggs and plants.

Gallimimus


Ceratosaurs

Ceratosaurs are huge carnivorous dinosaurs which existed from the Late Jurassic Period to the Late Cretaceous Period. The below dinosaurs are ceratosaurs.


Rugops

Rugops means "Wrinkle Face". It lived 95 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Tahore (Niger). It measured 19.7 ft long and weighed 750 kgs. Its delicate skull showed that it was a scavenger, not an attacking dinosaurs. It also had short arms which was used as counterbalance.

Rugops

Skorpiovenator

Skorpiovenator means "Scorpion Hunter". It lived 99.6 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Argentina. It measured 24.6 ft long and weighed 1.84 tons. It had short, almost useless arms but strong legs with powerful thighs for running. It arched its head backwards and then swung it down to dive its teeth to its prey's flesh.

Skorpiovenator

Rajasaurus

Rajasaurus means "Royal Lizard". It lived 72 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Gujarat (India). It measured 22 ft long and weighed 3 tons. The horn on the middle of its head is used for display or headbutting with other Rajasauruses. It was also used for defending its territiory.

Rajasaurus

Carnotaurus

Carnotaurus means "Carnivore Bull". It lived 113 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Patagonia (South America). It measured 28 ft long and weighed 1 ton. Its scales were like a collection of large pebble-like bumps. It had really small arms which could be the tiniest arms of any theropod. It had 4 fingers with sharp claws which was used to hold and tear its food.

Carnotaurus

Ceratosaurus

Ceratosaurus means "Horn Lizard". It lived 150 MYA in the Late Jurassic Period. Its fossils were found in Tanzania (Africa), Uruguay (South America), and Switzerland (Europe). It measured 29 ft long and weighed 520 kgs. It was the largest ceratosaur that ever lived. It had a large head with a short horn on the tip of its nose and horny bumps above its eyes. They were used for display to attract mates.

Ceratosaurus


Therizinosaurs

Therizinosaurs were dinosaurs who were herbivores, although they were theropods. They walked on 2 legs and had a tall neck. Some even had feathers! The following dinosaurs are therizinosaurs.


Beipiaosaurus

Beipiaosaurus means "Beipiao Lizard". It lived 125 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period. As the name implies, its fossils were found in Beipiao (China). It measured 7.2 ft long and weighed 45 to 91 kgs. It was the smallest therizinosaur that ever lived. Its neck was shorter but its head was larger when compared to other therizinosaurs. It had 2 coats of feathers. Its feathers were 10-15 cms long.

Beipiaosaurus

Nothronychus

Nothronychus means "Sloth-like Claw". It lived 89.3 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in New Mexico and Arizona (USA). It measured 14 ft long and weighed 800 kgs. It was a bulky herbivore with a sloth-like hip, elongated neck and arms with sharp claws measuring 30 cm long.

Nothronychus

Suzhousaurus

Suzhousaurus means "Suzhou Lizard". It lived 125 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period. As the name implies, its fossils were found in Suzhou (China) which is now known as Jiuquan. It measured 20 ft long and weighed 3.1 tons. It had a partial coating of feathers. It had long claws, a long neck, a large belly and a short tail.

Suzhousaurus

Segnosaurus

Segnosaurus means "Slow Lizard". It lived 86 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Mongolia (Asia). It measured 20-23 ft long and weighed 1.4 tons. It had a small head with a horny beak, and a long slender neck. They had large claws but they were not that long. It also had a large belly.

Segnosaurus

Therizinosaurus

Therizinosaurus means "Reaping Lizard". It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Mongolia (Asia). It measured 30-33 ft long and weighed 3-5 tons. It was the largest therizinosaur that ever lived. It also had the longest claws than any other dinosaur measuring 1 metre long. These claws were used for grabbing vegetation and to defend itself from other predators.

Therizinosaurus


Troodontids

Troodontids are a family of bird-like theropods. They were either herbivores, carnivores or omnivores. Just like the dromaeosaurs, they had sickle-shaped claws, but not as big as theirs. The following dinosaurs are troodontids.


Mei Long

Mei Long means "Sleeping Dragon". It lived 125 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in China (Asia). It measured 2 ft long and weighed 3.6 kgs. It was one of the dinosaurs with the shortest names. It ate small lizards and insects. It climbed trees to escape from predators. It also was a fast runner. The reason it got its name is because its first fossil showed it in a sleeping position. It would have killed by poisonous gas from a volcano.

Mei Long

Saurornithoides

Saurornithoides means "Bird-like Lizard" due to its bird-like skull. It lived 83.6 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Mongolia (Asia) and Nebraska (USA). It measured 6.6 to 9.8 ft long and weighed 13-27 kgs. It ran on its strong legs and used its long arms to seize and tear its prey. Its large eyes were positioned forwards so it could have binocular vision making it to judge distances and to make it nocturnal.

Saurornithoides

Troodon

Troodon means "Wounding Tooth". It lived 74 to 66 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in USA and Canada (North America). It measured 8 ft long and weighed 50 kgs. It ate plants, small mammals, lizards, snakes and birds and baby dinosaurs which existed at that time. It had large eyes to help it see and hunt in dusk or dawn. It had the largest brain than any other dinosaur, making it the most intelligent dinosaur. Its teeth were curved, very sharp and saw edged. It had 3 claws on each hand which was used for grabbing and holding its prey. It had long legs with a sicle-shaped claw on each foot. These claws were used for kicking into its prey to cause wounds and prevent it from being thrown off while using its teeth and claws to bite and tear.

Troodon

Latenivenatrix

Latenivenatrix means "Hiding Huntress" due to its bird-like skull. It lived 76-71 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Alberta (Canada). It measured 9.8 - 11.5 ft long and weighed 3 kgs. It was the largest troodontid that ever lived. It was an omnivore.

Latenivenatrix


Dilophosaurids

Dilophosaurids is a family of medium to large sized bipedal dinosaurs. They are well-known for their head crests which is used for attracting mates, to intimidate rivals and for thermoregulation. They lived in the Early Jurassic Period. They could even swim and catch fish. The following dinosaurs are dilophosaurids.


Dilophosaurus

Dilophosaurus means "Two Crested Lizard". It lived 190 MYA in the Early Jurassic Period. Its fossils were found in Arizona (USA). It measured 23 ft long and weighed 400 kgs. Its jaws were strong enough to puncture bones. It hunted in packs. Its hands were perfect for grasping an slashing prey. In the movie "Jurassic Park", it showed a Dilophosaurus being frill-necked and spitting venom. However, there was no evidence for such a thing in real life. So, it didn't have a frill and didn't spit venom.

Dilophosaurus

Dracovenator

Dracovenator means "Dragon Hunter". It lived 190.8 MYA in the Early Jurassic Period. Its fossils were found in South Africa (Africa). It measured 23 ft long and weighed 400 kgs. They were medium-sized, moderately-built bipedal carnivore. It ate prosauropods, which are the first sauropods to live.

Dracovenator


Tyrannosaurids

Tyrannosaurids is a family of huge theropod dinosaurs. They lived near the end of the Cretaceous Period and are found in mostly North America and Asia. They were apex predators. They had large skulls with large teeth. They had short arms with 2 fingers. Their heavy tails were used for balance. The following dinosaurs are tyrannosaurids.


Daspletosaurus

Daspletosaurus means "Frightful Lizard". It lived 75 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Canada and USA (North America). It measured 26-30 ft long and weighed 2 to 4.2 tons. It had a huge heavily-constructed skull. It was used to knock its prey unconscious. It coexisted with Gorgosaurus.

Daspletosaurus

Gorgosaurus

Gorgosaurus means "Dreadful Lizard". It lived 75 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Canada and USA (North America). It measured 26-30 ft long and weighed 3.1 to 3.2 tons. When its first fossil was discovered, it was thought to be a young Albertosaurus. However, later studies have shown that they were two different dinosaurs and the name "Gorgosaurus" was given. It coexisted with Daspletosaurus. Its scales were so small that its skin was almost smooth. It was faster when it was a youngster than when fully grown. This means its diet changed from fast orinithomimids to larger, but slower hadrosaurs.

Gorgosaurus

Albertosaurus

Albertosaurus means "Alberta Lizard". It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. As the name implies, its fossils were found in Alberta (Canada). It measured 32 ft long and weighed 4.4 tons. They hunted in packs. They had 60 conical-shaped sharp teeth in its jaws. It could reach 8-13 mph. It had two horns above its eyes to attract mates.

Albertosaurus

Tarbosaurus

Tarbosaurus means "Alarming Lizard". It lived 70 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in China and Mongolia (Asia). It measured 33-39 ft long and weighed 5 tons. It's also called as "Tyrannosaurus Bataar" or "T-Bataar". It had 58 to 64 teeth in its jaws. It was both a carnivore and a scavenger. It hunted large dinosaurs like hadrosaurs and titanosaurs.

Tarbosaurus

Tyrannosaurus Rex

Tyrannosaurus Rex means "Tyrant Lizard King". It lived 65 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in USA (North America) and Mongolia (Asia). It measured 40 ft long and weighed 8 tons. It was the largest tyrannosaurid that ever lived. It is called as T-Rex in short. It was a hunter, scavenger and a cannibal. It was an ambush hunter as well. It had powerful legs which helped it to run 30 mph. Although it had tiny arms, it was used to slash its prey. It had a good sense of sight, smell and hearing to find out where its prey is hiding. It also had a bite force which measured 12,000 psi!! So, a single bite of this predator can kill its victim. Their teeth were the size of bananas. It could rip off 230 kgs in one bite! Its brain was bigger than any plant eater. It was one of the most dangerous dinosaur at its time.

Tyrannosaurus Rex


Spinosaurids

Spinosaurids is a family of huge theropod dinosaurs. They lived in the Early Cretaceous Period. They had crocodile-like jaws which made them not only land-dwellers, but even fish! They were good swimmers as well. They had sails on their backs for thermoregulation, to attract mates and to frighten off enemies. They walked on two legs and sometimes on all fours. The following dinosaurs are spinosaurids.


Irritator

Irritator means "Irritating". It lived 100.5 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period Its fossils were found in Brazil (South America). It measured 26 ft long and weighed 3.6 tons. It got its name due to the feelings of the scientists when they found out that fossil dealers had added plaster to the front of the snout to make it more valuable. It had straight and cone-shaped teeth.

Irritator

Baryonyx

Baryonyx means "Heavy Claw". It lived 113 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period Its fossils were found in England and Spain (Europe). It measured 31 ft long and weighed 2.5 tons. It got its name because of its huge thumb claw which measured 30 cms long. These claws were used for stabbing and snatching fish from the water. It had 96 teeth on its jaw. These teeth were modified to hold its prey in place. It also held its head underwater and snatch fish with its crocodile jaw.

Baryonyx

Suchomimus

Suchomimus means "Crocodile Mimic". It lived 125 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period Its fossils were found in Niger (Africa). It measured 34-36 ft long and weighed 2.7 to 5.2 tons. It had 100 conical teeth, which are pointed but not sharp and slightly curved backwards. Its longest claw was perfect for hooking fish out of the water.

Suchomimus

Spinosaurus

Spinosaurus means "Spined Lizard". It lived 100 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period Its fossils were found in Egypt and Morocco (Africa). It measured 56 ft long and weighed 20 tons. It is the biggest of all the carnivores, even bigger than the T-Rex and Giganotosaurus which also meant it was the largest spinosaur. It was also one of the most fiercest predator. Its sail was made of bones measuring a maximum of 6 ft. It used its crocodile jaw to catch fish from water. However, it also used its hands for doing so, along with gripping and slashing its prey. It had paddled feet to help it to swim in water and catch fish. It also hunted crocodilians.

Spinosaurus


Carcharodontosaurids

Carcharodontosaurids is a family of huge theropod dinosaurs. They include some of the largest theropods. Some of them are even bigger than the T-Rex. The following dinosaurs are carcharodontosaurids.


Acrocanthosaurus

Acrocanthosaurus means "High-Spined Lizard". It lived 125 to 100 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period Its fossils were found in USA (North America). It measured 38 ft long and weighed 6.8 tons. The horns on top of its eyes was used for protection of the eyes. Its teeth were used for tearing meat from its prey and its arms with three fingers with a sickle-shaped claw on each were used for holding its prey. It got its name due to the spines on its back which ran from the neck to the front half of its tail. These spines were used for thermoregulation.

Acrocanthosaurus

Carcharodontosaurus

Carcharodontosaurus means "Shark-Tooth Lizard". It lived 100 MYA in the Early Cretaceous Period Its fossils were found in North Africa. It measured 42 ft long and weighed 6 tons. It got its name due to its shark-like teeth. It hunted in packs. It had long, muscular legs which made it run 19 mph. It could lift creatures with a maximum weight of 424 kgs with its jaws alone. It coexisted with Spinosaurus.

Carcharodontosaurus

Mapusaurus

Mapusaurus means "Earth Lizard". It lived 97 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period Its fossils were found in Argentina (South America). It measured 42 ft long and weighed 8 tons. It hunted in packs, so it could bring down large sauropods like Argentinosaurus.

Mapusaurus

Giganotosaurus

Giganotosaurus means "Giant Southern Lizard". It lived 97 MYA in the Late Cretaceous Period. Its fossils were found in Argentina (South America). It measured 50 ft long and weighed 14 tons. It was the largest carcharodontosaurid that ever lived. It was larger than the T-Rex. Its teeth were adapted to slice prey. Its three-clawed fingers were for slashing or grasping prey. It mostly hunted young or weak dinosaurs. It could hunt large sauropods like Argentinosaurus.

Giganotosaurus


Megalosaurids

Megalosaurids is a family of huge theropod dinosaurs. They all lived in the Jurassic Period. The following dinosaurs are megalosaurids.


Eustreptospondylus

Eustreptospondylus means "Well-Reversed Vertebrae". It lived 165 MYA in the Middle Jurassic Period. Its fossils were found in England (UK). It measured 20 ft long and weighed 0.5 tons. It was good at swimming and ate fish. It was also a hunter and scavenger. Since it lived in an island, it wasn't that big. It was the dinosaur with the most difficult name to pronounce.

Eustreptospondylus

Afrovenator

Afrovenator means "African Hunter". It lived 167 MYA in the Middle Jurassic Period. Its fossils were found in Niger (Africa). It measured 26 ft long and weighed 790 kgs. Its serrated teeth were used for slicing deeply into its prey's flesh and tearing it from their bodies. It used its long sickle-shaped claws on its fingers to grasp its prey and slash it open.

Afrovenator

Megalosaurus

Megalosaurus means "Great Lizard". It lived 166 MYA in the Middle Jurassic Period. Its fossils were found in Portugal, UK and France (Europe). It measured 30 ft long and weighed 700 kgs. It was the very first dinosaur to be named and the second d. It was named in 1824 nearly 150 years after its fossils were found!!!!

Torvosaurus

Torvosaurus means "Savage Lizard". It lived 153 - 148 MYA in the Late Jurassic Period. Its fossils were found in North America, Europe, Africa and South America. It measured 36 ft long and weighed 4-5 tons. It was the largest megalosaurid that ever lived. It was an ambush hunter as it could not run very fast.

Proceratosaurids

Proceratosaurids are a family theropod dinosaurs which lived from the Middle Jurassic to Early Cretaeous Period. The following dinosaurs are Proceratosaurids.


Gualong

Guanlong means "Crown Dragon". It lived 163.5 to 157.3 MYA in the Late Jurassic Period. Its fossils were found in China. It measured 9.8 ft in length and weighed 200 lbs. Its crest on its head is used for display. It also had feathers.
