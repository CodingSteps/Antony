Mahatma Gandhi

Early Life

Mahatma Gandhi was born on 2nd October 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat. His full name was Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi. His parents were Karamchand Uttamchand Gandhi and Putlibai Gandhi. His wife was Kasturbai Makhanji Kapadia. They were marrired on May 1883. His father died in 1885. Later, Gandhiji had 4 sons:-

  • Harilal, born in 1888
  • Manilal, born in 1892
  • Ramdas, born in 1897
  • Devdas, born in 1900

Life in London

One day, Gandhiji was advised to go to London for law studies. So, on 4th September 1888, he sailed to London and attended University College, London. He stayed there for 3 years and returned to India when he learned that his mother had died while he was in London and that his family had kept the news from him.


Life in South Africa


In April 1893, Gandhiji set sail for South Africa to be the lawyer of Abdullah's cousin. He spent 21 years (1893-1914) in South Africa, where he developed his political views, ethics and politics. Immediately when he arrived in South Africa, Gandhiji faced discrimination due to his skin colour and heritage, like all people of colour. He was discriminated in different ways like:-

  • He was not allowed to sit with European passengers in the stagecoach and was told to sit on the floor near the driver and then beaten up when he refused.
  • He was kicked into a gutter for daring to walk near a house.
  • He was thrown off a train at Pietermaritzburg when he refused to leave the first-class.
  • The magistrate of a Durban court ordered Gandhiji to remove his turban, which he refused to do.
  • Indians were not allowed to walk on public footpaths in South Africa. Gandhiji was kicked by a police officer out of the footpath onto the street without warning.

Struggle for Indian Independence

Gandhi returned to India in 1915. He brought an international reputation as a leading Indian nationalist, theorist and community organiser.Gandhi joined the Indian National Congress and was introduced to Indian issues, politics and the Indian people primarily by Gokhale.Gandhi took leadership of the Congress in 1920 and began escalating demands until on 26 January 1930 the Indian National Congress declared the independence of India. He was arrested 6 times:-

  1. On 10th April, 1919
  2. 10th March,1922
  3. 5th May, 1930
  4. 4th January, 1932
  5. 1st August, 1933
  6. 9th August, 1942

He fasted and protested until the British left India.

His assassin was Nathuram Vinayak Godse, an advocate of Hindu nationalism, a member of the political party - the Hindu Mahasabha, and a former member of the Hindu nationalist volunteer organisation Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS). Godse considered Gandhiji to have been too accommodating to Muslims during the Partition of India of the previous year. Before he was assasinated, Gandhiji was reaching the top of the steps leading to the raised lawn behind the Birla House where he had been conducting multi-faith prayer meetings every evening. As Gandhiji began to walk towards the dais, Godse stepped out and fired three bullets into his chest and abdomen. Gandhiji fell to the ground and died.
